LIFE BELOW WATER

made by memo, topen0330

Since the industrialization era,

seawater has become 26% more acidic

due to the rapid increase in carbon dioxide emissions.

Consequently,

the proportion of fish species at a biologically sustainable level

has drastically decreased 90% to 67%.

In South Korea,

the amount of waste collected in 2021 increased by a factor of five compared to 2008,

with 90% of it being plastic.

Given the gravity of this situation,

87 countries have come together to sign the Port State Action Agreement.

And since 2010,

the number of territorial waters designated as protected areas has doubled.

Why are they making such extensive efforts to preserve the ocean?

There are

about 212,000 species of marine animals,

also about 1.5 million animals live in the sea.

The destruction of such a vast marine ecosystem will not only harm the planet,

but will also make it impossible to recover from the damage caused by this negative impact.

Additionally,

the ocean absorbs a quarter of the carbon dioxide in the atmosphere,

which is highly beneficial in offsetting global warming.

However, as carbon dioxide levels rise,

oceans become acidified, leading to the loss of vital resources and harm to marine life.

As a result, the challenge of preventing global warming will become exceedingly difficult.

Additionally, the ocean offers substantial economic benefits to humans.

Scientists have found that designating marine sanctuaries

can boost tourism and bring economic advantages to nearby fisheries.

Thus, protecting the ocean is essential..

Then, What can we do in response?

First and foremost,

we need to reduce unnecessary plastic usage.

Reducing unnecessary plastic use is essential to combat ocean pollution.

Using reusable items like tumblers and eco bags helps significantly reduce plastic waste.

By making conscious choices to avoid single-use plastics,

we can prevent them from ending up in the ocean and harming marine life.

Making sustainable seafood choices is crucial for the health of our oceans.

Opting for seafood that has been responsibly sourced, such as tuna caught using the pole fishing method,

It is important to develop the habit of considering seafood purchases carefully.
